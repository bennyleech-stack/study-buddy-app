です (desu) is like the word "is/am/are" at the end of a sentence. ですか (desu ka) turns it into a question — the か (ka) is the question marker!

💡
わたしの (watashi no) means "my" — literally "I of". の (no) connects two nouns like "of" in English. So わたしのなまえ = "my name" = "name of me".

Key Phrases
Classroom requests, responses and full sentences
Classroom Requests — May I…?

All use the pattern: [action] + てもいいですか (te mo ii desu ka) = "May I…?"

なにかたべてもいいですか？
Nanika tabete mo ii desu ka?
May I eat something?
みずをとりにいってもいいですか？
Mizu wo tori ni itte mo ii desu ka?
May I go get some water?
トイレにいってもいいですか？
Toire ni itte mo ii desu ka?
May I go to the toilet?
おんがくをきいてもいいですか？
Ongaku wo kiite mo ii desu ka?
May I listen to music?
えんぴつをかりてもいいですか？
Enpitsu wo karite mo ii desu ka?
May I borrow a pencil?

Self Introduction Template
はじめまして。
Nice to meet you.
→
わたしのなまえは ＿＿＿ です。
My name is ___.
→
どうぞよろしく。
Pleased to meet you.
💡
てもいいですか is your permission-asking superpower. Break it down: て (te) = doing the action, も (mo) = even, いい (ii) = good/fine, です (desu) = is, か (ka) = question. All together: "Is it fine even if I do [action]?"
