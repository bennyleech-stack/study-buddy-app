Short Answer Questions
SECTION F
Q20 · Short Answer
Name three properties of particles described in the particle model of matter.
Model Answer: Any three of: (1) All matter is made of particles. (2) Particles are always moving. (3) Particles are attracted to each other. (4) The arrangement and movement of particles changes depending on the state of matter (solid, liquid, gas).
Q21 · Short Answer
A mixture of sand, iron filings, and salt is dissolved in water. Describe the steps you would take to separate all three components. State which technique you use at each step and why.
Model Answer:
Step 1 — Magnetism: Use a magnet to remove the iron filings, because iron is magnetic and sand/salt are not.
Step 2 — Filtration: Pour the remaining sand + saltwater through filter paper. The sand is too large to pass through; the saltwater passes through as the filtrate.
Step 3 — Evaporation: Heat the saltwater until the water evaporates (particles gain energy and escape as gas), leaving behind solid salt crystals.
Q22 · Short Answer
Explain what happens at a particle level when a liquid evaporates.
Model Answer: When a liquid is heated, particles near the surface gain enough energy to overcome the attractions holding them in the liquid. These high-energy particles escape from the surface and become gas particles, spreading out into the air. As more particles escape, the liquid slowly disappears.
